Commuter Line Traffic

Overview
I conducted a traffic analysis of the Jabodetabek Commuter Line using one day’s worth of trip data.
From 1,210 trips, I identified usage patterns, most popular destinations, and peak-hour trends — all visualized in an easy-to-understand dashboard.

Data Source :
Kaggle

1. Data Visualization with Power BI

A. DASHBOARD

B. KEY INSIGHTS
  • ✅ Total Trips: 1,210
  • ✅ Unique Destinations: 190
  • ✅ Peak Hour: 08:00–09:00 (morning rush)
  • ✅ Most Popular Destination Station: Jakartakota (190 trips)



C. BREAKDOWN BY COMMUTER LINE
  • ✅ Bogor Line → 383 trips (31.65%) — the highest!
  • ✅ Cikarang Line → 357 trips (29.5%)
  • ✅ Rangkasbitung Line → 204 trips (16.86%)


Note: The Bogor and Cikarang corridors are the main commuter routes into Jakarta.

D. TRIPS PER HOUR
  • ✅ Morning peak (08:00–09:00): 78 trips
  • ✅ Evening peak (17:00–18:00): 72 trips
  • ✅ After 22:00: Sharp decline — only 17 trips at 01:00


Note: Mobility is highly influenced by office hours, with a steep drop after nighttime.

E. TOP DESTINATION STATIONS
  • ✅ Jakartakota — 190 trips
  • ✅ Bogor — 164 trips
  • ✅ Kampung Bandan — 123 trips


Note: Jakartakota serves as a major transit hub — likely due to its connectivity to business centers and other public transport systems.

F. COMMUTER LINE TRIP DISTRIBUTION BY ROUTE
  • The Bogor Line dominates with over 31% of total trips, indicating it is the busiest and most critical corridor for daily commuters between Bogor and Jakarta.
  • The Cikarang Line ranks second at nearly 30%, highlighting high demand from eastern satellite cities like Bekasi and Cikarang.
  • Rangkasbitung and Tangerang Lines also show significant usage — reflecting the importance of southern and western transport links into Jakarta.


Note: Prioritize increasing train capacity on the Bogor and Cikarang Lines during peak hours to reduce overcrowding.

2. Recommendation

Based on our analysis, there are some recommendation.
  • Prioritize increasing train capacity on the Bogor and Cikarang lines during peak hours.
  • Improve facilities and crowd management at Jakartakota Station, the busiest destination.
  • Evaluate train frequency after 22:00 — potential for operational efficiency through reduced service.
